## Data Authority and Limitations

### Source Authority

This Tool aggregates data from official Costa Rican legal databases. However:

| Source Type | Authority Level | Use Case |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| **SCIJ (pgrweb.go.cr)** | Authoritative | Statute text, codes, regulations (primary reference) |
| **La Gaceta (Diario Oficial)** | Authoritative (promulgation) | Official publication of laws and decrees |
| **Community/open-data sources** | Supplementary | Cross-references, metadata (verify independently) |
| **Treaty databases** | Authoritative for international law | International obligations, SICA agreements |

**CRITICAL**: Community-maintained resources are **NOT** official government publications. While generally reliable, they may contain transcription errors, outdated information, or incomplete coverage.

### Commercial Legal Databases

For professional legal work, **always cross-check with commercial legal databases and official repositories** such as:

- SCIJ (official and free), Nexus PJ, legal publishers specializing in Costa Rican law

These services provide editorial oversight, comprehensive annotations, and professional-grade currency guarantees that this Tool **cannot** provide.

## Currency and Staleness

### No Real-Time Updates

- Database updates are **manual** and may lag official publications by **weeks or months**
- Costa Rican law changes continuously through new statutes (leyes), decrees (decretos ejecutivos), regulations (reglamentos), and court decisions (sentencias)
- **Last-updated timestamps** in tool responses indicate data age, but should be treated as unreliable

### Staleness Warnings

The Tool includes staleness warnings when data is >30 days old. **These warnings are not exhaustive**:

- Even "fresh" data may be outdated if recent amendments were published in La Gaceta (Diario Oficial)
- Staleness warnings do not guarantee completeness or accuracy
- Always check official sources for the current legal position (derecho vigente)

### Amendments and Repeals

- A provision marked "in force" may have been:
  - Amended (reformado) after the last database update
  - Repealed (derogado) but not yet removed from the database
  - Subject to transitional rules (disposiciones transitorias) affecting its applicability
  - Declared unconstitutional by the Sala Constitucional (Sala IV)
- The Tool does **NOT** automatically track amendments in real-time

## Coverage Gaps

### What's Missing

This Tool does **NOT** include:

1. **Complete case law** -- Coverage of Corte Suprema de Justicia, Sala Constitucional, and lower court decisions is limited; do not rely solely on this for case law research (investigación jurisprudencial)
2. **Historical statute versions** -- Limited availability of historical provision wording (versiones anteriores)
3. **Legal commentary** -- No editorial annotations, academic doctrine (doctrina), or practice guides
4. **Unpublished decisions** -- Lower court decisions (sentencias de tribunales inferiores) and administrative rulings largely absent
5. **Full legislative history** -- Limited coverage of legislative debates (actas de la Asamblea Legislativa) and explanatory materials (exposiciones de motivos)

### Impact on Professional Use

These gaps mean:

- **Incomplete Legal Research**: Tool results are inherently incomplete and may miss critical authorities
- **Context Missing**: Without doctrine and legislative history, interpretation may be incorrect
- **International Law Blind Spots**: Cannot fully assess SICA integration obligations, CAFTA-DR provisions, or other international treaty compliance
